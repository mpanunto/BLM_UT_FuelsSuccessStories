On September 17th 2024 a lightning strike ignited the Prairie wildfire in the Moab Field Office, near the Colorado border. Prolonged dryness, unseasonably high temperatures, and winds from 20-30 mph all contributed to the rapid spread of the fire through grass and sagebrush. In less than 12 hours the fire had burned 1920 acres and threatened multiple structures.

To mitigate fire danger from cheatgrass monocultures and help establish existing perennial bunch grasses, the Canyon Country Fuels program performed the 4,739 ac Bitter Creek Aerial Herbicide treatment in 2019. The project covered recent fire scars in the bitter creek vicinity. The herbicide used was Plateau, a pre-emergent herbicide that prevents the germination of new grass seeds while leaving established plants and most seeds unaffected.

Herbicide treatments had a measurable effect in reducing cheatgrass cover. In the first 2 years after treatment it brought cover to nearly 0%. However, as time goes on the presence of cheatgrass re-establishes. In the case of the Prairie Fire, 5 years after the herbicide treatment, cheatgrass cover was still reduced compared to untreated plots and that difference was sufficient to stop the head of the fire. Retreatment of herbicide may be required to maintain the effectiveness of hazardous fuels reduction.
